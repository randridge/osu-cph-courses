\exercise{3C}{A small sample of undergraduate students was randomly selected from a chemistry course. Their GPAs were recorded and are shown below. Use these data to calculate the requested summary measures.

GPAs: 3.08, 2.5, 3.33, 3.75, 3.1, 2.64

(a) Standard deviation

\answer{6cm}{ANSWER: 0.456\\
~\\
Mean: $\bar{x}=3.07$ (calculated in Practice Exercise 3B.2)\\
\begin{tabular}{lcc}
$x_i$ & $x_i-\bar{x}$ & $(x_i-\bar{x})^2$\\ \hline
3.08	& 0.01	& 0.0001\\
2.5	& -0.57	& 0.3249\\
3.33	& 0.26	& 0.0676\\
3.75	& 0.68	& 0.4624\\
3.1	& 0.03	& 0.0009\\
2.64	& -0.43	& 0.1849\\ \hline
\multicolumn{2}{r}{Sum:} & 1.0408 \\
\multicolumn{2}{r}{Divide by 5 to get $s^2$:} & 0.208 \\
\multicolumn{2}{r}{$s=\sqrt{s^2}$:} & 0.456 \\
\end{tabular}
}

(b) Coefficient of variation

\answer{3cm}{ANSWER: 0.149\\
~\\
Mean: $\bar{x}=3.07$ (calculated in Practice Exercise 3B.2)\\
Standard deviation: $s=0.456$ (part (a) above)\\
$\displaystyle cv = \frac{s}{\bar{x}}=\frac{0.456}{3.07}=0.149$
}

(c) 75th percentile

\answer{1.5cm}{ANSWER: 3.33\\
~\\
Sort data values: 2.5, 2.64, 3.08, 3.1, 3.33, 3.75\\
$nk/100 = 6\times75/100 = 4.5$ = not an integer, so round up to next integer $\rightarrow$ 5\\
5th observation (in order) = 3.33}
}
